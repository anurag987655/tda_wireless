Sine Wave Signal

Signal Parameters: ω = 2π·5, Sampling Rate = 200Hz, Period = 40 samples.

• τ = 10 represents a perfect quarter-period (T/4 = 90° phase shift). Topologically, the 2D delay embedding forms a perfect, non-intersecting circle, producing a highly persistent 1D cycle ($H_1$).
• τ = 20 is a half-period (T/2 = 180° phase shift). The embedding collapses into a straight line (since $x_{t+\tau} = -x_t$), destroying the loop and causing zero persistent 1D cycles in the diagram.

Discrete Amplitude / Periodic

Square Wave Signal

Signal Parameters: ω = 2π·5, Sampling Rate = 200Hz, Period = 40 samples.

• Because the square wave alternates strictly between amplitudes +1 and -1, the delay embedding coordinates $(x_t, x_{t+\tau})$ dwell entirely at four corner points: (1, 1), (1, -1), (-1, 1), (-1, -1).
• As a result, the embedding consists of clustered points at these coordinates. In the persistence diagram, we see the components merge into a single component ($H_0$) and no high-persistence $H_1$ cycles, representing the discontinuous discrete-amplitude nature of the signal.

Phase Shift Modulation / Continuous & Discontinuous

Binary Phase Shift Keying (BPSK) Signal

Signal Parameters: 5 bits, 100 samples/bit, Carrier Frequency f_c = 5Hz, Carrier Period = 20 samples.

• Carrier & Phase Shift Dynamics: The carrier wave forms the main oscillation (with a period of 20 samples). BPSK shifts the phase of the carrier by 180° to represent a digital bit shift (0 vs 1).
• Topological Impact of τ:
  • τ = 5 (Quarter-period shift $T/4$) yields circular rings representing the carrier. Transitions between phase states form intersecting paths crossing the center.
  • τ = 10 (Half-period shift $T/2$) forces the carrier loop to collapse onto the diagonal line $x_{t+\tau} = -x_t$.
  • τ = 20 (Full-period shift $T$) forces the carrier to align onto the main diagonal $x_{t+\tau} = x_t$.
• Persistence Signature: The combination of continuous carrier cycles and abrupt $180^\circ$ phase reversals creates multiple overlapping cycles. The persistence diagrams show a complex structure of short-to-medium-lived $H_1$ cycles, mapping the transition trajectories.
